MESSAGE FROM UNIVERSITY POLICE
PUBLIC SAFETY NOTICE

Dear OSU Community Member:

You are receiving this notice because a crime has occurred that is considered to be of a concern and/or a continuing threat to the campus community.

The purpose of this message is to heighten your safety awareness by providing community members with information necessary to take appropriate precautions, enable you to take actions to help increase your safety, and to aid in the prevention of similar crimes.

This notice does not mean there has been an increase in crime or that any specific crime pattern exists.

Public Safety Notice for:
Campus Area, Off Campus Area, Medical Center, Residence Halls

Date: 2013-09-26

Alert Code: 2013-7

The following crime occurred on campus:
The Ohio State University Police Division received a report of an armed robbery at the Huntington Bank located at 235 W. 11th Avenue 9:35 a.m. today. A previous armed robbery was reported at the same location at 9:25 a.m. on Saturday, 9-14-13. The suspect description for the first robbery was of a lone black male, 25-35 years of age, 5'08" to 5'10" tall, weighing 150-170 lbs. This suspect had a medium build, short brown hair, was wearing a plaid shirt, blue jeans, a black hat, and sunglasses and was armed with a knife. The second robbery suspect was described as a lone black male of similar physical description. This suspect was wearing tan pants, a black Adidas jacket with white stripes on each sleeve, a ball cap, and he was armed with a handgun. OSU Police and FBI are investigating. Suspect photos from the first incident are available at http://dps.osu.edu/. Photos from the second will be added as soon as they are available. This Public Safety Notice is being issued to heighten awareness that the suspect information is similar, suggesting a pattern and to ask for assistance from the university community in gathering information about his whereabouts.

Anyone with information concerning this crime should contact either the University Police, 292-2121 or Columbus Police, 645-4545. You may also report information anonymously to the Central Ohio Crime Stoppers at 461-TIPS or the University Crime Stoppers Tips line at 247-TIPS.
