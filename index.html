Buckeye Alert Text Messaging System Upgrade

Buckeye Alert Text Messaging System Upgrade!


Ohio State Emergency Management is continually looking for better ways to communicate with our campus community in an emergency. We currently offer 2 dozen different methods from which we can communicate with some or all of our campus community.

We are excited to announce that on July 1, 2013, the Buckeye Alert text messaging system will be receiving an upgrade. Our new system will provide new and improved ways to communicate with all our students, faculty, staff, parents and other community members.

There is no action on your part to remain in the system. If your cell number is in our Student Information System (BuckeyeLink) or PeopleSoft (Faculty/Staff) then you are automatically registered for the Buckeye Alert Text Messaging System.

With our new system, we hope to improve the speed with which we can get text messages to the campus community. In the coming months, we will be looking at new methods to add to our “toolbox” that will help us communicate better and faster in an emergency.

One more change that you will notice. Beginning July 1, 2013, the Buckeye Alert system will be activated for all tornado WARNINGS. A warning means that a tornado has been sighted. Notifications of tornado warnings will include a text message, OSU Emergency Management Facebook (OSUEmergency) and OSU Emergency Management Twitter (@OSU_EMFP).

If you have any questions, please contact us via email at emergencymanagement@osu.edu
